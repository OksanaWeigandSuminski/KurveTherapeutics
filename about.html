KURVE THERAPEUTICS

Kurve Therapeutics, (Lynnwood, Washington and Manhattan, NY) is a specialty-drug company focused on next-generation treatments for neurodegenerative diseases. Current therapies for these disorders, especially complex molecules such as monoclonal antibodies and larger peptides, have trouble crossing the blood brain barrier (BBB). Kurve’s patented technology creates a precisely-controlled turbulent flow that navigates the curved, complex geometry of the nose and accesses the most-penetrable part of the BBB at the top of the nasal cavity.

Kurve products have had remarkable, disease-modifying clinical success, improving the lives of Alzheimer’s patients in five double-blind, placebo-controlled Phase II studies. The program is ready for Phase III. In addition, Kurve patients with Lewy body dementia, Parkinson’s and ALS have also experienced sustained improvements in quality of life. Kurve’s devices are also involved in research at the US Department of Defense for PTSD and Traumatic Brain Injury. A full list of studies is available on request.

The company was founded by a chronic sinusitis sufferer determined to cure what repeated attempts (including several surgeries) could not. Extensive research and development yielded the first device successful at penetrating the entire nasal cavity as well as the paranasal sinuses. Nose-to-brain delivery, a long-elusive “holy grail” for treatment of CNS diseases, was the next, obvious target. The first success was demonstrated by Kurve devices administering intranasal insulin for Alzheimer’s disease.

The drugs used in Kurve devices for neurodegenerative diseases (e.g., Alzheimer’s disease, cognitive aging, Lewy body dementia, Parkinson’s disease and ALS) are available in generic form. Kurve Therapeutics therefore possesses all of the relevant intellectual property and in effect owns these breakthrough treatments in neurodegenerative diseases. Clinical success has already been demonstrated for each of these diseases. Case files for Alzheimer’s and Parkinson’s patients provide notable, personal examples; further details please see Patient cases under Results.

There are now seventeen peer-reviewed publications of trials using Kurve nose-to-brain delivery technology, with further publications pending. The company has recent or ongoing collaborations with several leading research centers, including Harvard, Johns Hopkins and the Karolinska Institute.
