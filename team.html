MANAGEMENT TEAM

Marc is founder and CEO of Kurve Therapeutics. He is the inventor of record for 26 existing and pending patents underpinning Kurve’s intellectual property. Marc has won industry awards and been interviewed in multiple industry publications, some of which made Kurve the focus of covers and articles. Marc has been invited to be a speaker at industry expos and conferences and more recently has chaired a number of industry round tables. The thrust of his professional life was dedicated to the high-tech manufacturing sector where he conceived and developed new products. His designs in efficiency equipment saved his clients millions in production costs. Prior to founding Kurve in 2002, Marc was President of EES, Inc., a high-tech staffing organization that improved businesses through identifying, cultivating and consulting to effective executive teams.

Dr. Cornett has spent the past decade developing drugs for psychiatric and neurological indications, including a drug about to enter clinical trials for autism. Previously, he founded Navitas Pharma, a company repurposing a French hypertension drug for new uses in the US, taking the company through a successful exit 3.5 years later. Before that, he worked at McKinsey, Eli Lilly, Razorfish (where he ran the strategy practices in the US and Europe) and Los Alamos National Laboratory. He has a neuroscience PhD from UCLA and an MD with Distinction in Research from the University of Michigan. He has a black belt in karate and has run 14 marathons.

Ms. Park has extensive strategy, enterprise transformation and growth experience, at Accenture, partner firms and the Massachusetts Governor’s healthcare council. She has led healthcare clients to develop new market segments, driving growth of 100 to 300%. She has worked with hedge funds and ventures; has a Yale MBA and Amherst College BA with Sloan fellowships.

Tom’s financial background spans operating management and investment banking. He is a CPA who began his career in auditing and consulting with Arthur Young (now Ernst & Young) in Los Angeles. He went on to manage Financial Reporting at 20th Century Fox and then became Assistant Controller at Orion Television. He helped to found Windswept Capital in Seattle where he served as EVP and CFO. During his tenure at Windswept, he led a team that built a successful mergers and acquisitions firm serving middle-market companies in the Pacific Northwest. Tom earned a BS in Business Administration from the University of Southern California.
